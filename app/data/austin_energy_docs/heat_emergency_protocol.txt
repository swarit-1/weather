Austin Energy Heat Emergency Protocol

When the heat index exceeds 105°F, Austin Energy activates its Heat Emergency Protocol to manage increased electrical load and protect public safety.

Load Management:
- Demand response programs are activated when system load exceeds 95% of available capacity.
- Voluntary conservation appeals are issued through media and social channels.
- Large commercial customers on interruptible tariffs may be curtailed.
- Transformer loading is monitored hourly; units exceeding 120% nameplate are flagged for immediate relief.

Grid Operations:
- Transmission operators increase spinning reserves to account for potential generation trips.
- Distribution feeder switching plans are prepared to redistribute load from overloaded transformers.
- Capacitor banks are verified operational to maintain voltage support during peak load.

Public Safety:
- Cooling centers are opened in coordination with the City of Austin.
- Austin Energy customer assistance programs provide bill relief for vulnerable populations.
- Outdoor workers are reminded of OSHA heat illness prevention standards.
- Crews rotate on 2-hour cycles with mandatory hydration and rest breaks when heat index exceeds 110°F.

Equipment Protection:
- Transformer oil temperatures are monitored via SCADA; alarm thresholds are lowered during heat events.
- Underground cable ampacity is de-rated by 10% when soil temperatures exceed thresholds.
- Substation ventilation and cooling systems are verified operational before peak hours.

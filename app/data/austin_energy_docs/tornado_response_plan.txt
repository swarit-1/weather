Austin Energy Tornado Response Plan

Tornado warnings represent the highest threat level for Austin Energy infrastructure. Immediate protective actions are required when the National Weather Service issues a Tornado Warning for any portion of the service territory.

Immediate Actions:
- All field personnel take shelter immediately upon tornado warning issuance.
- System operators prepare for widespread outage by pre-loading restoration switching plans.
- Emergency Operations Center is activated with full staffing.
- Mutual aid requests are initiated proactively; do not wait for damage assessment.

Damage Assessment:
- Aerial patrol of transmission lines begins within 1 hour of tornado warning expiration.
- Ground crews inspect substations in the warning polygon before energizing.
- Customer outage reports are mapped against the tornado track for prioritized restoration.

Restoration Priority:
1. Transmission lines serving multiple substations.
2. Substations serving critical facilities (hospitals, water, emergency services).
3. Distribution feeders with highest customer count.
4. Individual service restorations.

Public Safety Considerations:
- Downed power lines are presumed energized until verified de-energized by qualified personnel.
- Austin Energy coordinates with Austin Fire Department for downed line response.
- Public messaging emphasizes staying away from downed lines and reporting them to 311.
- Generators connected without transfer switches create backfeed hazards; crews must verify isolation before working lines.

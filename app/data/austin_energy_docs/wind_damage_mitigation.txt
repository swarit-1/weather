Austin Energy Wind Damage Mitigation Guidelines

High wind events are a leading cause of power outages in the Austin Energy service territory. These guidelines outline preventive and response measures for wind-related grid impacts.

Prevention:
- Vegetation management program maintains clearances per NERC FAC-003 standards.
- Distribution poles are inspected on a 10-year cycle; poles failing strength tests are replaced.
- Guy wires and anchors on transmission structures are inspected annually.
- Smart reclosers are configured to lock out after three attempts during sustained high winds to prevent equipment damage.

Operational Thresholds:
- Wind gust 35 mph: Heightened awareness; crews monitor conditions.
- Wind gust 45 mph: Crew standby; suspend non-emergency overhead work.
- Wind gust 60 mph: Full emergency activation; all crews sheltered until winds subside.

Outage Response:
- Outage Management System automatically prioritizes restoration by customer count and critical facility impact.
- Fuse coordination ensures upstream devices do not trip on downstream faults.
- SCADA-operated switches are used to isolate faulted sections and restore unfaulted segments.

Post-Event Review:
- All wind-related outages are analyzed for root cause (tree contact, equipment failure, foreign object).
- Chronic outage locations are flagged for infrastructure hardening (undergrounding, stronger poles, spacer cable).
- Lessons learned are incorporated into the annual storm preparedness plan.
